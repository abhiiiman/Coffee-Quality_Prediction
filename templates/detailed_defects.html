Blackened Beans

Blackened beans are caused by over-roasting or exposure to excessive heat during processing. They result in a burnt flavor and bitterness, negatively impacting the taste of the coffee.

Sour Beans

Sour beans develop a sharp, unpleasant taste due to fermentation during processing. This defect leads to acidity and sourness in the coffee, affecting its overall flavor profile.

Under-ripe Beans

Under-ripe beans are not fully developed and lack sweetness. They contribute to a sour and grassy taste in the coffee, diminishing its flavor quality.

Over-ripe Beans

Over-ripe beans are overly mature and may exhibit a fermented or alcoholic taste. They can cause off-flavors and spoil the overall taste experience of the coffee.

Moldy Beans

Moldy beans develop mold due to improper storage or exposure to moisture. They impart a musty and unpleasant flavor to the coffee, making it undrinkable.

Insect-Damaged Beans

Insect-damaged beans have been infested by pests, resulting in physical damage and a sour or musty taste. They compromise the quality and safety of the coffee.

Fermented Beans

Fermented beans undergo excessive fermentation, leading to a strong, vinegar-like taste. This defect ruins the flavor balance and aroma of the coffee.

Flat Beans

Flat beans lack the characteristic shape and texture of normal coffee beans. They produce a weak and insipid brew with little aroma or flavor.

Chipped Beans

Chipped beans have fractured or broken surfaces, exposing the inner bean to oxidation and degradation. They contribute to a bitter and unpleasant taste in the coffee.

Broken Beans

Broken beans are fragmented or shattered into smaller pieces, increasing surface area and accelerating spoilage. They result in a lower-quality brew with a harsh and astringent flavor.

Stinker Beans

Stinker beans emit foul odors due to bacterial contamination or fermentation. They impart a rotten or putrid taste to the coffee, rendering it undrinkable.

Hull Fragments

Hull fragments are remnants of the coffee cherry hull left on the bean after processing. They may add unwanted bitterness and astringency to the coffee if not removed properly.

Mottled Beans

Mottled beans have uneven coloration or spotting, indicating inconsistent ripening or processing. They result in a brew with uneven flavor and texture, reducing overall quality.
